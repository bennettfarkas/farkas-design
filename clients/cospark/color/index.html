The CoSpark palette is drawn from materials, not a color wheel. Every color is something you can touch — oxidized metal, quarried stone, weathered patina, fired clay.

Primary

Copper and Slate carry 80% of everything — they are the brand's signature. Copper is warm, activating, optimistic. Slate is grounding, trustworthy, institutional without being institutional. Together they create the warm-cool tension that gives CoSpark its visual energy.

Usage Guidelines

Rules for consistent, intentional color usage across all touchpoints.

Primary Ratio

Copper and Slate carry 80% of brand expression. They should dominate every composition — other colors support, never compete.

Copper Usage

Copper is for action, energy, and CTAs. Use it to draw the eye and signal interactivity. Never set body text in Copper.

Slate as Anchor

Slate is the default text and background color. It grounds the page and provides the neutral canvas that lets Copper and accent colors shine.

Secondary Role

Patina and Ember are supporting players — use them for accents, data visualization, status indicators, and illustrations. They add range without diluting the primary pair.

High Contrast Pairing

Copper and Patina sit as near-complements. Their pairing creates electric contrast — use it deliberately for maximum visual impact.

Accessibility

Always maintain WCAG AA contrast (4.5:1 minimum) for text. Use the scale steps to find accessible pairings — light text on dark backgrounds, dark text on light.
